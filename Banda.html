Galle - Sri-Lanka

Discover Galle Galle is a charming coastal city in Sri Lanka, known for its rich history, stunning architecture, and beautiful beaches. The highlight of Galle is the UNESCO-listed Galle Fort, a historic fortress built by the Portuguese in the 16th century and later fortified by the Dutch. Within the fort's walls, you'll find narrow streets lined with colonial buildings, boutique shops, cafes, and museums.
Galle offers a perfect blend of cultural heritage and natural beauty. Visitors can explore ancient churches, mosques, and temples, stroll along the fort's ramparts with panoramic ocean views, and relax on nearby pristine beaches. Whether you're a history buff, a foodie, or a beach lover, Galle promises an unforgettable experience.

Kandy - Sri-Lanka

Kandy, nestled in the lush hills of central Sri Lanka, is a city of rich cultural heritage and stunning natural beauty. Known as the cultural capital of Sri Lanka, Kandy is home to the sacred Temple of the Tooth Relic, which houses a revered relic of the Buddha and is a major pilgrimage site for Buddhists around the world.
Surrounded by verdant tea plantations and picturesque landscapes, Kandy offers a serene escape with its cool climate and tranquil ambiance. The city is also famous for its annual Esala Perahera festival, a vibrant and colorful parade featuring traditional dancers, drummers, and beautifully adorned elephants.

Badulla - Sri-Lanka

Badulla, a serene town nestled in the central highlands of Sri Lanka, is known for its lush green landscapes, picturesque tea plantations, and rich cultural heritage. As the capital of the Uva Province, Badulla offers a tranquil escape from the hustle and bustle of city life.
The town is home to several historical and natural attractions, including the famous Dunhinda Falls, a majestic waterfall surrounded by dense forest. Another notable site is the Muthiyangana Temple, an ancient Buddhist temple that is one of the Solosmasthana, the 16 sacred places in Sri Lanka.
With its cool climate, scenic beauty, and cultural landmarks, Badulla is a perfect destination for those looking to experience the natural and historical charm of Sri Lanka. See more.....

Nine Arch Bridge

The Nine Arch Bridge, also known as the "Bridge in the Sky," is a stunning feat of engineering located in Ella, Sri Lanka. This iconic railway bridge, built during the British colonial period, spans 91 meters and stands 24 meters high, gracefully curving through the lush greenery of the hill country.
Constructed entirely of brick, stone, and cement without the use of steel, the bridge is an architectural marvel and a testament to the ingenuity of its builders. The surrounding area is equally breathtaking, with tea plantations, dense jungle, and misty hills providing a picturesque backdrop.
The Nine Arch Bridge is a popular spot for both locals and tourists.

Sigiriya

Sigiriya, often referred to as the "Lion Rock," is one of Sri Lanka's most iconic landmarks and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. This ancient rock fortress and palace complex is located in the Matale District, surrounded by lush forests, gardens, and water features.
Rising nearly 200 meters (660 feet) above the surrounding landscape, the massive rock column is topped by the remnants of a once-magnificent palace. Visitors can climb a series of staircases to reach the summit, passing by the famous "Lion's Gate" and ancient frescoes known as the "Sigiriya Maidens."
At the top, breathtaking panoramic views of the surrounding countryside await,
